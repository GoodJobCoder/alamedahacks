Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers! Here are the most common questions about Alameda Hacks.

Alameda Hacks is open to all high school students currently living in Alameda County. You must be enrolled in high school to participate.
Teams can have a maximum of 4 people. You can also participate solo if you prefer, or we can help match you with other participants looking for teammates.
While some programming knowledge is helpful, it's not required! We welcome beginners and will provide resources and mentorship to help you learn and build something amazing.
You can build anything you want! Web applications, mobile apps, games, hardware projects, or any creative tech solution. The theme and specific challenges will be announced at the start of the hackathon.
Yes! Alameda Hacks is completely free to participate. We provide all the resources, mentorship, and prizes at no cost to participants.
We'll use Discord for communication, collaboration, and mentorship. You'll receive all the necessary links and instructions after registration.
The exact dates will be announced soon! Make sure to register to stay updated on all announcements and schedule details.
Projects will be judged based on creativity, technical implementation, presentation, and impact. More detailed judging criteria will be provided before the event.
